<h2>Visa Related Questions</h2>
<ol>
<li id='what_is_a_visa'>
<h3 class='h4'>What is a visa?</h3>
<p>U.S. Citizens and lawful permanent residents do not need to obtain a visa to enter the United States. In general, a citizen of a foreign country who seeks to enter the United States must first obtain a U.S. visa. Certain international travelers may be eligible to enter the United States without a visa if they meet the requirements for the U.S. Visa Waiver program. For further information, please visit the Department of State website at <a href="https://travel.state.gov" target="_blank">https://travel.state.gov</a>.</p>
</li>
<li id='what_types_of_visa'>
<h3 class='h4'>What types of visas are available?</h3>
<p>There are various types of nonimmigrant visas for temporary travel to the United States. The type of visa you need depends on your purpose of travel. For information on all types of visas please visit <a href="https://travel.state.gov/content/travel/en/us-visas/visa-information-resources/all-visa-categories.html" target="_blank">https://travel.state.gov/content/travel/en/us-visas/visa-information-resources/all-visa-categories.html</a>.</p>
</li>
<li id='c1d_b1b2_dual_visa'>
<h3 class='h4'>I wish to apply for C1/D and B1/B2 dual visa. What should I do?</h3>
<p>You should complete one online DS-160 application form and pay one visa fee in order to schedule an appointment for your C1/D visa.</p>
<p>At the time of your interview, you should inform Consular Officer that you wish to apply for a C1/D and B1/B2 dual visa.</p>
<p>Note: Applying for dual visa is for C1/D, no other nonimmigrant visa combination is acceptable.</p>
</li>
<li id='who_decides_if_i_get_a_visa'>
<h3 class='h4'>Who decides if I will get a visa?</h3>
<p>Under U.S. law the Department of State has responsibility for issuing visas, and most visas are issued at one of the Department of State Embassies and Consulates abroad. A U.S. Consular Officer will review your application at the time of the interview and decide whether you are qualified for a visa.</p>
<p>A visa doesn't permit entry to the U.S. A visa simply indicates that your application has been reviewed by a U.S. Consular Officer at US Embassy or Consulate, and that the officer determined you are eligible to travel to the port-of-entry for a specific purpose.</p>
<p>Neither CSRA nor any of our staff play any part or influence in the outcome of your visa application.</p>
</li>
<li id='how_long_to_process'>
<h3 class='h4'>How long does it take to process my visa application?</h3>
<p>The time it will take for the Consular Section to process your visa application can vary depending on: </p>
<ul>
<li>the completeness of your visa application</li>
<li>the amount of verification your visa application requires can affect the amount of time it take to process your application</li>
<li>Consular Section may find that you need to provide further information before a decision can be made on your application</li>
</ul>
<p>If you wish to view the estimate on how long you will have to wait to get an interview appointment, please visit the U.S Department of State online tool at <a href="https://travel.state.gov/content/travel/en/us-visas/visa-information-resources/wait-times.html" target="_blank">https://travel.state.gov/content/travel/en/us-visas/visa-information-resources/wait-times.html</a>. This online tool will also tell you how long it will take for your visa to be processed at the Consular Section, after a decision is made by a Consular Officer to issue the visa. Please note that the processing time does not include any time required for administrative processing.</p>
</li>
<li id='admin_processing'>
<h3 class='h4'>What is &quot;administrative processing&quot;?</h3>
<p>Some visa applications require further administrative processing, which takes additional time after the visa applicant's interview by a consular officer. Applicants are advised of this requirement at the time of the consular officer interview. After attending the interview, you may track the status of your visa application at <a href="https://ceac.state.gov/CEAC" target="_blank">https://ceac.state.gov/CEAC</a> or, if applicable, follow any local instructions given to you at the interview. For more information on administrative processing or for case specific inquiries, please visit <a href="https://travel.state.gov/content/travel/en/us-visas/visa-information-resources/administrative-processing-information.html" target="_blank">https://travel.state.gov/content/travel/en/us-visas/visa-information-resources/administrative-processing-information.html</a> or the Consular Section website.</p>
</li>
<li id='how_long_can_i_stay'>
<h3 class='h4'>How long am I authorized to stay in the United States?</h3>
<p>Applicants should be aware that a visa does not guarantee entry into the United States. Legally, the visa specifies the period during which the bearer may apply at the port-of-entry to enter the United States.</p>
<p>At the port-of-entry, a U.S. immigration officer of the Department of Homeland Security (DHS) decides whether to allow you to enter and how long you can stay for any particular visit, as part of the Admission process. Only the U.S. immigration officer has authority to permit you to enter the United States. For further information, please see "Admission to the United States and your Duration of Stay" <a href="https://travel.state.gov/content/travel/en/us-visas/visa-information-resources/visa-expiration-date.html" target="_blank">https://travel.state.gov/content/travel/en/us-visas/visa-information-resources/visa-expiration-date.html</a>.</p>
</li>
<li id='apply_two_visa_types_same_time'>
<h3 class='h4'>Can I apply for two different visa types at the same time?</h3>
<p>This service allows the applicant to pay for and schedule one visa appointment at a time.</p>
<p>The Consular Section may allow the applicant to interview and pay for a second visa type during the scheduled appointment for the first visa type.</p>
<p><strong>Procedure for applying for two separate visa types at the same time:</strong></p>
<ol>
<li>Use this website or telephone service to pay the MRV fee and schedule an appointment for one of the visa types for which you want to apply.</li>
<li>Attend your scheduled appointment(s).</li>
<ul>
<li>Be sure to bring all application documentation for the scheduled visa type.</li>
<li>In addition, be sure to bring additional documents and payment for the second (unscheduled) visa type.</li>
<li>You must indicate both visa types on your DS-160 at <a href="https://ceac.state.gov/genniv" target="_blank">https://ceac.state.gov/genniv</a>.</li>
</ul>
<li>At the scheduled appointment, follow the Consular Officerâ€™s instructions to complete the application process for the second visa type, including payment of the MRV fee.</li>
</ol>
</li>

<h2>Visa Renewals and Extensions Questions</h2>
<ol>
<li id='extend_visa'>
<h3 class='h4'>How can I extend my visa?</h3>
<p>If you want to extend your temporary stay in the United States, you must file a request with U.S. Citizenship and Immigration Services (USCIS) before your authorized stay expires. If you remain in the United States longer than authorized, you may be barred from returning and/or you may be removed (deported) from the United States. Check the period of stay that was stamped on your passport by Customs and Border protection officials to determine the date your authorized stay expires. To learn more how to extend your visa, please visit USCIS website atÂ <a href="https://www.uscis.gov/visit-united-states/extend-your-stay" target="_blank">https://www.uscis.gov/visit-united-states/extend-your-stay</a>.</p>
</li>
<li id='expiring_visa'>
<h3 class='h4'>I have a nonimmigrant visa that will expire soon. Do I need to go through the whole visa application process again?</h3>
<p>Yes, you will need to go through the entire visa application process each time you apply for a visa, even if your visa is still valid. However, those who are renewing a previous U.S. visa or who meet certain other age-related criteria may qualify for a waiver of the visa interview. Eligibility is determined based on the answers provided to questions during the appointment scheduling process. However, consular officers reserve the right to require an interview for any visa applicant.</p>
</li>
</ol>

<h2>Passport Questions</h2>
<ol>
<li id='passport_blank_page'>
<h3 class='h4'>Why do I need a blank page in my passport?</h3>
<p>U.S. nonimmigrant visas require an entire passport page to be printed. Please assure that your passport contains at least 1-2 blank visa/stamp pages for the printing of the visa and the associated entry stamps.</p>
</li>
<li id='dual_citizen_which_passport'>
<h3 class='h4'>I have dual citizenship. Which passport should I use to travel to the United States?</h3>
<p>If you have dual citizenship and are not a U.S. Citizen, you must select whichever nationality you prefer to enter the United States. You must enter and depart the United States using one passport.</p>
</li>
<li id='valid_visa_passport_stolen'>
<h3 class='h4'>My passport with the valid visa was stolen. What should I do?</h3>

To learn more, see Lost and Stolen Passports, Visas, and Form I-94s at <a href="https://travel.state.gov/content/travel/en/us-visas/visa-information-resources/lost-stolen-visas.html" target="_blank">https://travel.state.gov/content/travel/en/us-visas/visa-information-resources/lost-stolen-visas.html</a>.</p>

<li id='passport_validity_length'>

<li id='passport_replacement'>
<h3 class='h4'>I had to replace my passport before the scheduled appointment date. What do I need to do?</h3>
<p>The applicant can attend the scheduled appointment and present the new passport. If the applicant is not able to obtain a new passport before the scheduled appointment date, the applicant must cancel or reschedule the appointment. An applicant will not be able to apply for a visa without a valid passport. Please provide a copy of the police report if the passport was stolen.</p>
</li>
<li id='expired_passport_valid_visa'>
<h3 class='h4'>My passport has expired but my U.S. visa is still active. What do I need to do?</h3>
<p>Unless canceled or revoked, a visa is valid until its expiration date. If your visa is still valid you can travel to the United States with your two passports, as long as the visa is valid, not damaged, and is the appropriate type of visa required for your primary purpose of travel. Both passports (the valid and the expired one with the visa) should be from the same country and type. Please note: A valid visa is not guarantee of entry into the United States. Final determination of entry is at the sole discretion of the CBP Officer reviewing the documentation.</p>
</li>
